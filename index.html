Abstract

FarmTalk is an innovative hybrid solution addressing critical challenges in plant disease identification and remediation in agriculture. By combining YOLOv8, a state-of-the-art object detection model, with GPT-3.5 integrated with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), the system offers real-time disease detection from leaf images and provides reliable, context-specific remediation advice. This approach aims to overcome limitations of traditional methods and LLM hallucinations, potentially revolutionizing crop management and enhancing food security.

Results

User Query: what is wrong with the plant image? and how do i fix it?
Generated Prompt: The leaf in the appears to be affected by a fungal disease commonly known as rust. This type of fungal infection is caused by various species of fungi. The control measures are:

  • Pruning and Sanitation: Remove and dispose of affected leaves and plant debris to reduce the spread of spores.
  • Chemical Control: Apply fungicides if the infection is severe. Fungicides containing active ingredients like chlorothalonil, mancozeb, or sulfur can be effective. Follow label instructions for safe application.
  • Improving Air Circulation: Avoid overcrowding plants, as rust thrives in high-humidity conditions. Better air circulation can reduce fungal growth.
  • Watering Practices: Water the plant at the base rather than overhead to prevent leaf wetness, which promotes rust spore germination.




Conclustion and Future Work

In conclusion, the FarmTalk project presents a promising approach to enhancing plant disease management by integrating real-time disease detection with reliable remediation advice through a hybrid model that combines YOLOv8 and GPT-3.5 with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). This solution addresses the limitations of traditional methods, offering farmers a tool that not only identifies plant diseases quickly but also provides context-specific treatment recommendations, thereby improving agricultural efficiency and sustainability. Current limitation will include, limited disease coverage, as of now the model only covers plants such as cotton, and corn. More datasets need to be collected to increase model database and capabilities. One potential harm/risk to society would be a over dependence on such tools to replace traditional on-site experiance and knowledge, which would not be desirable. This can be addressed by acknowledging that this is a tool to make things conveniant and expedite action and not a replacement.
